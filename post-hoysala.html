Hoysala Architecture of Karnataka

Home Blog Hoysala Bengal

Introduction

The Hoysala temples of Karnataka are known for their star-shaped layouts, intricate carvings, and polished soapstone pillars. Built between the 11th and 14th centuries, these temples are masterpieces of medieval Indian art.

Key Features

  • Star-shaped ground plans with zig-zag walls
  • Soapstone carvings that preserve intricate details
  • Lathe-turned pillars
  • Rich sculptural narratives depicting gods, dances, and daily life

Famous Temples

Chennakesava Temple, Belur: Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, famous for its star-shaped plan and detailed carvings.

Hoysaleswara Temple, Halebidu: Twin-shrine temple dedicated to Lord Shiva with 400 reliefs narrating epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata.

Significance

The Hoysala style expresses regional identity, creativity, and devotion, setting it apart from other medieval Indian architectural traditions.
