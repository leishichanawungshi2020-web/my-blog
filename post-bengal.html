Introduction

The Bengal Sultanate (13th–16th centuries) developed a unique architectural style using brick, terracotta, and curved roofs, adapted to the region’s heavy monsoons and humid climate.

Key Features

  • Brick construction with terracotta decoration
  • Bangla curved roof inspired by rural huts
  • Domes and arches for ventilation and structural support
  • Terracotta ornamentation depicting floral motifs and daily life scenes

Famous Monuments

Adina Mosque, Pandua: One of India’s largest mosques, combining Persian and Central Asian forms with local brickwork.

Sixty-Dome Mosque, Bagerhat: Built by Khan Jahan Ali, featuring 77 domes on 60 pillars and optimized for Bengal’s humid climate.

Significance

The Bengal Sultanate style demonstrates cultural fusion, climatic adaptation, and regional identity, distinct from stone-based monuments in other parts of India.
